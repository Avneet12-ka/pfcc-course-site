                <h3>Case Study 2: Pediatric ICU Family Involvement Model</h3>
                
                <div class="case-overview">
                    <h4>Overview</h4>
                    <p>This case study examines how Children's National Medical Center in Washington, DC transformed their Pediatric Intensive Care Unit (PICU) practices to fully integrate families as active participants in care and decision-making.</p>
                </div>
                
                <h4>Background</h4>
                <p>Children's National PICU is a 44-bed unit serving critically ill children from the Washington metropolitan area and beyond. Prior to their PFCC initiative, the unit had restrictive visiting policies and limited family involvement in care processes.</p>
                
                <h4>PFCC Transformation</h4>
                <div style="margin: 1.5rem 0;">
                    <h5>Key Elements of the PICU Family Involvement Model:</h5>
                    <ul>
                        <li><strong>Family Presence Policy:</strong> Eliminated traditional "visiting hours" in favor of 24/7 family access, with accommodations for overnight stays</li>
                        <li><strong>Family-Centered Rounds:</strong> Structured daily rounds to include families as active participants, not just observers</li>
                        <li><strong>Parent Education Program:</strong> Comprehensive education to prepare parents for participation in care activities</li>
                        <li><strong>Parent Support Network:</strong> System connecting experienced PICU families with newly admitted families</li>
                        <li><strong>Family Participation Menu:</strong> Tool allowing families to indicate their desired level of involvement in specific care activities</li>
                        <li><strong>Family-Staff Communication Boards:</strong> Bedside tools to enhance information sharing and goal alignment</li>
                    </ul>
                </div>
                
                <div style="background-color: #f0f7ff; padding: 1.5rem; border-radius: 8px; margin: 1.5rem 0; border-left: 4px solid #1a56db;">
                    <h4>Implementation Process</h4>
                    <p>The PICU transformation followed a structured change management approach:</p>
                    <ol>
                        <li><strong>Assessment:</strong> Conducted surveys and focus groups with families and staff to identify gaps in family-centered care</li>
                        <li><strong>Planning:</strong> Formed a multidisciplinary team including parents to design the new model</li>
                        <li><strong>Education:</strong> Provided comprehensive training for all PICU staff on new processes and family engagement skills</li>
                        <li><strong>Implementation:</strong> Phased rollout of individual components with continuous refinement</li>
                        <li><strong>Evaluation:</strong> Systematic measurement of impact on families, patients, staff, and clinical outcomes</li>
                    </ol>
                </div>
                
                <h4>Overcoming Challenges</h4>
                <p>The implementation team encountered several challenges:</p>
                <ul>
                    <li><strong>Staff Concerns:</strong> Some staff initially worried about increased workload, interference with care, and privacy issues</li>
                    <li><strong>Space Constraints:</strong> The physical environment needed modifications to accommodate family presence</li>
                    <li><strong>Cultural Barriers:</strong> Diverse family backgrounds required adaptations to the standard approach</li>
                    <li><strong>Workflow Disruption:</strong> Existing clinical workflows needed redesign to incorporate family participation</li>
                </ul>
                
                <p>These challenges were addressed through:</p>
                <ul>
                    <li>Staff champions from each discipline who modeled and promoted new practices</li>
                    <li>Creative space utilization and minor renovations</li>
                    <li>Cultural competence training and multilingual resources</li>
                    <li>Gradual workflow adjustments with staff input</li>
                </ul>

                <div style="display: grid; grid-template-columns: repeat(auto-fit, minmax(250px, 1fr)); gap: 1rem; margin: 1.5rem 0;">

                        <h5>Family Outcomes</h5>
                        <ul>
                            <li>Increased family satisfaction (92% very satisfied)</li>
                            <li>Decreased family anxiety and stress</li>
                            <li>Improved family preparedness for home care</li>

                        <h5>Clinical Outcomes</h5>
                        <ul>
                            <li>25% reduction in medication errors</li>
                            <li>Improved pain management</li>
                            <li>Decreased length of stay by 0.8 days</li>
                        </ul>

                        <h5>Staff Outcomes</h5>
                        <ul>
                            <li>Increased job satisfaction after initial adjustment period</li>
                            <li>Improved collaboration with families</li>
                            <li>Enhanced cultural competence</li>
                        </ul>

                <h4>Background</h4>
                <p>The original NICU at Ampang Hospital was an open-bay design with 30 beds in a shared space, limited privacy, high noise levels, and minimal accommodations for parents. When planning a renovation, leadership committed to incorporating PFCC principles into the design process.</p>
                
                <h4>Family-Centered Design Process</h4>
                <div style="margin: 1.5rem 0;">
                    <h5>Key Elements of the Design Process:</h5>
                    <ul>
                        <li><strong>Design Committee:</strong> Formation of a committee including architects, clinical staff, and former NICU parents</li>
                        <li><strong>Family Experience Mapping:</strong> Documentation of the family journey through the NICU to identify pain points and opportunities</li>
                        <li><strong>Family Focus Groups:</strong> Multiple sessions with diverse families to gather input on design priorities</li>
                        <li><strong>Staff-Family Design Workshops:</strong> Collaborative sessions to develop and refine design concepts</li>
                        <li><strong>Prototype Testing:</strong> Mock-ups of key spaces that families and staff could experience and evaluate</li>
                        <li><strong>Post-Occupancy Evaluation:</strong> Structured assessment after implementation to identify further improvements</li>
                    </ul>
                </div>
                
                <h4>Design Features Based on Family Input</h4>
                <div style="display: grid; grid-template-columns: repeat(auto-fit, minmax(300px, 1fr)); gap: 1.5rem; margin: 1.5rem 0;">
                    <div style="background-color: #e6fffa; padding: 1.5rem; border-radius: 8px;">
                        <h5><i class="fas fa-bed" style="color: #38a169; margin-right: 0.5rem;"></i>Private/Semi-Private Rooms</h5>
                        <p>Single or double patient rooms with space for parents to stay overnight, providing privacy, noise control, and individualized environments</p>
                    </div>
                    <div style="background-color: #e6fffa; padding: 1.5rem; border-radius: 8px;">
                        <h5><i class="fas fa-home" style="color: #38a169; margin-right: 0.5rem;"></i>Family Lounge Areas</h5>
                        <p>Comfortable spaces where parents can rest, prepare meals, connect with other families, and access educational resources</p>
                    </div>
                    <div style="background-color: #e6fffa; padding: 1.5rem; border-radius: 8px;">
                        <h5><i class="fas fa-baby" style="color: #38a169; margin-right: 0.5rem;"></i>Kangaroo Care Spaces</h5>
                        <p>Dedicated areas with comfortable chairs and privacy screens for skin-to-skin holding, with appropriate equipment nearby</p>
                    </div>
                    <div style="background-color: #e6fffa; padding: 1.5rem; border-radius: 8px;">
                        <h5><i class="fas fa-pray" style="color: #38a169; margin-right: 0.5rem;"></i>Cultural Considerations</h5>
                        <p>Design elements respecting Malaysia's diverse religious and cultural practices, including prayer rooms and culturally appropriate dcor</p>
                    </div>
                    <div style="background-color: #e6fffa; padding: 1.5rem; border-radius: 8px;">
                        <h5><i class="fas fa-phone-alt" style="color: #38a169; margin-right: 0.5rem;"></i>Communication Technology</h5>
                        <p>Video conferencing systems allowing families unable to be present to see their baby and speak with the care team</p>
                    </div>
                    <div style="background-color: #e6fffa; padding: 1.5rem; border-radius: 8px;">
                        <h5><i class="fas fa-graduation-cap" style="color: #38a169; margin-right: 0.5rem;"></i>Parent Education Center</h5>
                        <p>Space dedicated to parent training and education, with multimedia resources in multiple languages</p>
                    </div>
                </div>
                
                <h4>Implementation Challenges</h4>
                <ul>
                    <li><strong>Budget Constraints:</strong> Balancing ideal design elements with financial limitations</li>
                    <li><strong>Space Limitations:</strong> Working within the existing building footprint</li>
                    <li><strong>Staffing Model Adjustments:</strong> Adapting nursing workflow from open bay to private room design</li>
                    <li><strong>Technology Integration:</strong> Ensuring new monitoring systems functioned reliably in the redesigned space</li>
                    <li><strong>Cultural Sensitivity:</strong> Accommodating diverse cultural and religious practices in the design</li>
                </ul>
                
                <h4>Outcomes</h4>
                <div style="margin: 1.5rem 0;">
                    <h5>Quantitative Outcomes:</h5>
                    <ul>
                        <li>30% increase in parental presence at the bedside</li>
                        <li>40% increase in kangaroo care hours</li>
                        <li>25% reduction in nosocomial infection rates</li>
                        <li>20% reduction in average length of stay</li>
                        <li>18% improvement in staff satisfaction scores</li>
                        <li>Significant reduction in ambient noise levels (65dB to 45dB)</li>
                    </ul>
                    
                    <h5>Qualitative Outcomes:</h5>
                    <ul>
                        <li>Parents reported feeling more respected, comfortable, and involved in care</li>
                        <li>Staff noted improved communication with families and higher family engagement</li>
                        <li>Families from diverse backgrounds reported feeling their cultural needs were respected</li>
                        <li>Improved transition to home reported by families</li>
                    </ul>
                </div>
