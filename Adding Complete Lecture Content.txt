                <div class="slide-content">
                    <h2 class="slide-title">Core Communication Skills in PFCC</h2>
                    <div class="slide-body">
                        <p>Effective PFCC requires mastery of specific communication skills that build trust and partnership.</p>
                        
                        <div style="background-color: #f0f7ff; padding: 1.5rem; border-radius: 8px; margin: 1.5rem 0;">
                            <h4>Essential Communication Skills</h4>
                            <ul class="slide-list">
                                <li><strong>Active Listening:</strong> Fully focusing on the speaker, demonstrating attention, and providing feedback</li>
                                <li><strong>Plain Language:</strong> Using jargon-free, clear communication appropriate to health literacy levels</li>
                                <li><strong>Teach-Back Method:</strong> Asking patients to explain information in their own words to verify understanding</li>
                                <li><strong>Empathetic Response:</strong> Acknowledging emotions and demonstrating understanding</li>
                                <li><strong>Non-verbal Communication:</strong> Being aware of body language, facial expressions, and physical positioning</li>
                            </ul>
                        </div>
                        
                        <p>Research shows that healthcare providers typically interrupt patients within the first 11-18 seconds of their speaking. Active listening means allowing patients to fully express their concerns before responding.</p>
                    </div>
                </div>
            </div>

                    <h2 class="slide-title">Cultural Considerations in Communication</h2>
                    <div class="slide-body">
                        <p>Cultural factors significantly influence healthcare communication and must be considered in PFCC implementation.</p>
                        
                        <div style="display: grid; grid-template-columns: repeat(2, 1fr); gap: 1.5rem; margin: 1.5rem 0;">
                            <div style="background-color: #f0f7ff; padding: 1rem; border-radius: 8px;">
                                <h4>Cultural Dimensions Affecting Communication</h4>
                                <ul>
                                    <li><strong>Collectivism vs. Individualism:</strong> How decisions are made within family and community structures</li>
                                    <li><strong>Power Distance:</strong> How authority and hierarchy influence provider-patient interactions</li>
                                    <li><strong>Communication Style:</strong> Direct vs. indirect communication preferences</li>
                                    <li><strong>Time Orientation:</strong> Different perspectives on punctuality, scheduling, and future planning</li>
                                </ul>
                            </div>
                            <div style="background-color: #f0f7ff; padding: 1rem; border-radius: 8px;">
                                <h4>Effective Cross-Cultural Communication</h4>
                                <ul>
                                    <li>Assess your own cultural biases and assumptions</li>
                                    <li>Ask about communication preferences</li>
                                    <li>Use professional interpreters when needed</li>
                                    <li>Validate understanding across cultural differences</li>
                                    <li>Adapt your approach based on cultural context</li>
                                </ul>
                            </div>
                        </div>
                        
                        <p>A culturally competent approach avoids stereotyping while recognizing the influence of culture on healthcare expectations and communication patterns.</p>
